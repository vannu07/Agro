KRISHI MITR: CROP RECOMMENDATION AND ENVIRONMENTAL THRESHOLDS GUIDE
===================================================================

Precision agriculture depends on matching soil characteristics and environmental parameters to the biological needs of specific crops. The Crop Recommendation Agent utilizes a multi-variable Random Forest model to analyze nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), potassium (K), pH, temperature, relative humidity, and rainfall.

1. NUTRIENT RATIOS (N-P-K) AND CROP SUITABILITY
------------------------------------------------
- Rice (Chawal): Requires high nitrogen for vegetative growth and moderate phosphorus/potassium.
  * Optimal N-P-K: N (80-100), P (40-60), K (40-60) kg/hectare.
  * pH Range: 5.5 - 6.5 (prefers slightly acidic soils).
  * Temperature: 20°C - 30°C.
  * Rainfall: 1500 - 2000 mm.

- Wheat (Gehun): Requires solid nitrogen and balanced phosphorus for root development.
  * Optimal N-P-K: N (100-120), P (50-60), K (40) kg/hectare.
  * pH Range: 6.0 - 7.5 (neutral soil).
  * Temperature: 15°C - 25°C.
  * Rainfall: 600 - 800 mm.

- Maize (Makka): A heavy feeder requiring extensive nitrogen.
  * Optimal N-P-K: N (120), P (60), K (40) kg/hectare.
  * pH Range: 5.8 - 6.5.
  * Temperature: 21°C - 27°C.
  * Rainfall: 500 - 800 mm.

- Cotton (Kapaas): Requires moderate nitrogen and phosphorus, and high potassium for fiber strength.
  * Optimal N-P-K: N (60-80), P (40), K (40-60) kg/hectare.
  * pH Range: 6.0 - 7.5.
  * Temperature: 25°C - 35°C.
  * Rainfall: 500 - 750 mm.

- Pulse Crops (Dal/Legumes): Capable of atmospheric nitrogen fixation, requiring low nitrogen application but high phosphorus.
  * Optimal N-P-K: N (20-30), P (40-60), K (30) kg/hectare.
  * pH Range: 6.0 - 7.0.
  * Temperature: 20°C - 30°C.
  * Rainfall: 400 - 600 mm.

2. PARAMETRIC IMPORTANCE IN SOIL METRICS
----------------------------------------
- Nitrogen (N): Drives leaf development and chlorophyll synthesis. Shortages lead to chlorosis (yellowing of leaves).
- Phosphorus (P): Critical for root architecture, flower initiation, and seed formation. Shortages lead to stunted growth and purple-tinted stems.
- Potassium (K): Regulates stomatal opening, water retention, and disease resistance. Shortages lead to leaf margin necrosis.
- Soil pH: Determines nutrient availability. In highly acidic soils (pH < 5.0), elements like phosphorus and calcium become locked, while aluminum toxicity rises. In alkaline soils (pH > 8.0), iron and zinc become unavailable.

Precision matching ensures optimized crop yield and safeguards soil longevity.
