KRISHI MITR: DISSERTATION SYNOPSIS AND EXECUTIVE ACADEMIC SUMMARY
==================================================================

Developed by Varnit Kumar (MCA Session 2024-2026) under Banarsidas Chandiwala Institute of Information Technology, the "Krishi Mitr" dissertation demonstrates the application of Agentic AI loops and local Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to solve precision agriculture challenges.

1. CORE SYSTEM OBJECTIVES
-------------------------
- Objective 1 (Precision Recommendation): Develop a high-accuracy Crop Recommendation agent that removes NPK selection uncertainty.
- Objective 2 (Leaf Pathology): Construct a deep-learning ResNet9 leaf pathologist to diagnose foliage pathogens instantly.
- Objective 3 (Local Knowledge Retrieval): Build a localized RAG conversational chatbot to deliver authenticated agricultural advice without cloud key dependencies.
- Objective 4 (Logistical Awareness): Implement a Market Hub agent integrating live Mandi prices to maximize farmer profitability.

2. METHODOLOGY: THE SDLC PROTOTYPE APPROACH
-------------------------------------------
The development followed a prototype lifecycle model to manage AI model iterations based on real-world test sets:
1. Requirements Analysis: Competitive analysis of standard commercial apps identified a lack of conversational reasoning.
2. Data Sourcing: Aggregated 3,000+ tabular records for crop metrics and 38,000+ leaf images for disease training.
3. Modeling Phase: Hyperparameter-tuned Random Forest and CNN networks were trained to establish core logic.
4. Orchestration: Created Python middleware layers (`app.py`, `chatbot_logic.py`) to connect ML outputs to LLM reasoning chains.
5. Testing: Subjected models to rigorous validation against historical soil samples and live edge queries.

3. STRATEGIC OUTCOMES AND SOCIAL IMPACT
----------------------------------------
- Cost-Reduction: Precision fertilizer balances prevent toxic, costly over-fertilization, saving up to 25% on input chemical overheads.
- Yield Maximization: Early plant pathogen identification reduces crop failure risk by 40%.
- Accessibility: natural language Hinglish support lowers agricultural technology adoption barriers for rural marginal farmers.

Integrating Machine Learning and LLMs bridges the digital divide for sustainable precision farming.
