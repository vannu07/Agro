KRISHI MITR: WEATHER RESILIENCE AND CLIMATE ADAPTATION GUIDE
============================================================

Unpredictable weather is the single greatest risk to open-field farming. Climate change has led to unseasonal rainfall, intense heatwaves, and late-winter frost. Implementing proactive resilience strategies safeguards crop investments.

1. MITIGATING UNSEASONAL AND HEAVY RAINFALL
-------------------------------------------
- Surface Drainage: Ensure fields are graded with a minor slope. Create perimeter drainage channels to quickly divert excess runoff away from crop roots.
- Raised Bed Planting: Construct raised soil beds (15-30 cm high) for crops like Maize, Cotton, and Vegetables. If heavy rain falls, excess water sits in the furrows while root crowns remain elevated, avoiding waterlogging and damping-off disease.
- Fungicidal Cover: Wet leaves in warm weather trigger fungal spores. Apply preventative organic copper sprays immediately after unseasonal storms.

2. COMBATING HEATWAVES AND THERMAL STRESS
-----------------------------------------
- Kaolin Clay Spray: During peak summer heatwaves, spray a 2-4% suspension of kaolin clay on foliage. This leaves a reflective white film, lowering leaf temperature by 2-4°C and reducing transpiration water loss without blocking photosynthesis.
- Micro-Sprinklers: Run overhead sprinklers for 10-15 minutes during the hottest afternoon hours. This increases local humidity and lowers ambient temperature, preventing blossom drop in vegetables.
- Straw Mulching: Cover soil surfaces with crop residue or dry leaves. Mulch insulates soil, keeping root zone temperatures cool and stable.

3. WINTER FROST PREPAREDNESS
----------------------------
- Frost (Pala): Can freeze plant sap, bursting plant cell walls.
- Mitigation:
  * Late-Evening Irrigation: Water holds heat better than dry soil. Giving fields a light watering on forecasted frost nights raises soil temperature, insulating roots.
  * Smoke generation: Burn dried straw and organic waste on the windward side of the field. The resulting smoke blanket traps heat radiating from the ground, preventing temperature drop.

Climate adaptation turns meteorological threats into manageable operational events.
